Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between PNG and JPG?

PNG is a lossless format that supports transparency, while JPG is a lossy format that doesn't support transparency but typically has smaller file sizes. JPG is better for photographs, while PNG is better for graphics with transparency needs.

Will converting PNG to JPG reduce quality?

JPG uses lossy compression, so some quality loss is inevitable. However, with higher quality settings (80-100%), the difference is often barely noticeable while still achieving significant file size reduction.

Can I convert transparent PNGs to JPG?

Yes, but transparent areas will become white in the JPG since JPG doesn't support transparency. If you need to maintain transparency, consider converting to PNG-24 instead.

Is there a limit to the number of PNGs I can convert?

You can convert multiple PNGs at once, but very large batches may cause performance issues. For best results, we recommend converting up to 20 images at a time.

Are my images stored on your servers?

No, all image processing happens directly in your browser. Your files never leave your device.
