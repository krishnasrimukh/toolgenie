Frequently Asked Questions

How does the word counter work?

The word counter splits your text into words based on spaces and punctuation. It counts each distinct word, character, sentence (based on punctuation), and paragraph (based on line breaks).

Does it count hyphenated words as one or two words?

Hyphenated words (like "state-of-the-art") are counted as a single word. This is the standard used by most publishers and word processors.

How is reading time calculated?

Reading time is calculated by dividing the word count by words-per-minute (wpm). The default is 200 wpm (average adult reading speed), but you can adjust this in the options.

Does the counter work with different languages?

Yes, the counter works with any language that uses spaces between words. For languages without spaces (like Chinese or Japanese), character count will be accurate but word count may not be.

Is there a limit to how much text I can count?

No, you can count text of any length. The tool processes everything in your browser, so there are no server-side limits.
