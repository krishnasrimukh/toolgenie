Frequently Asked Questions

What is a QR code?

A QR code (Quick Response code) is a type of barcode that can be read by smartphones and other devices to quickly access information like websites, text, or contact details.

How do I scan a QR code?

Most modern smartphones have built-in QR code scanners in their camera apps. Simply open your camera app and point it at the QR code. If your device doesn't support this, you can download a QR code scanner app.

What's the difference between the error correction levels?

Higher error correction means the QR code can still be scanned even if part of it is damaged or obscured, but it also makes the QR code more complex. For most uses, Medium (15%) is sufficient.

Can I use colored QR codes?

Yes, you can customize both the QR code color and background color. Just ensure there's enough contrast between the colors for reliable scanning.

Is there a limit to how much data a QR code can hold?

QR codes can store up to about 3KB of data, but shorter content (like URLs) work more reliably. Very complex QR codes may be harder to scan.
